Temasek Foundation Specialists' Community Action and Leadership (TF SCALE) Exchange Programme 2014

Temasek Foundation (TF) and three of Singapore’s polytechnics – Nanyang Polytechnic (NYP), Republic Polytechnic (RP), and Singapore Polytechnic (SP) – have partnered with 10 technical and vocational education and training (TVET) colleges from across ASEAN to launch the Temasek Foundation Specialists’ Community Action and Leadership Exchange (TF SCALE), a student leadership exchange program. Temasek Foundation has committed a grant of about S$1 million towards the program.

A total of 300 students – comprising 150 from Singapore and 150 from Cambodia, Indonesia, Philippines, Thailand, and Vietnam – participated in the inaugural run of TF SCALE.

For the inbound portion programme, NYP, RP and SP each hosted 50 students from around the region. During the four-week programme in Singapore, the TF SCALE scholars engaged in various TVET-related academic programmes and participated in a series of workshops where they worked in teams to apply technical skills and knowledge to develop social innovation projects with their Singaporean counterparts.

As the Technical Leader for the inbound portion of the program, my responsibility was to conduct workshops introducing students to the Raspberry Pi, managing inventory, and supporting students by guiding and pointing them towards the right direction for their social innovation projects which solve real-world problems.

Following this, the 150 TF SCALE scholars from Singapore (50 from each of the three polytechnics) were attached to one of the regional TVET institutions for a period of two weeks. Here, the Singapore scholars joined their regional counterparts to implement their co-developed social innovation projects to benefit the local communities.

As the Overall Leader for the outbound portion of the programme, my resposibility was to plan, support, and assist both the "Social" and "Technical" teams in Malang, Indonesia.

The Social Team's conducted an excersion to the nearby Orphanage in the slums to donate and interact with the children for a day.

The Technical Team continued work with the students from Polytechnic of Malang with their projects, from testing to deploying the devices.

Backend Developer for Team Florescence

This is a final year project involving 6 students (3 Software Developers and 3 Business Analyst) in collaboration with Urban Redevelopment Authority.

Flourishing our Locale aims to create an analytics dashboard to allow these businesses to obtain greater insights into their customers' sentiments by consolidating reviews, updating periodically, allow businesses to react to shifting sentiments, and improve the overall experience.

The main deliverables of the project include an analytics dashboard for each individual user that allows them to view their customer's sentiments, word cloud (Verb-Noun Pair), and sentiments over time. As well as, an administrative dashboard to have an overview of the precinct that allows administrators to view the spatial limits of the precincts, ranking (normalized), customer's sentiments over time, and word cloud. And easily compare between subzones.

My main responsibilities for this project include.

  • System architecture design
  • Programming analytics functions into programming objects and interfaces
  • Implementing programming principles (DRY, KISS, Single Responsibility Model, etc.)
  • Improving the complexity/efficiency of the program.
  • Backend Application Programming Interfaces for data retrieval
